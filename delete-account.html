What Data We Hold

When you use Money Merge — especially with Google Sign-in — the following data may be stored on our servers:

  • Firebase Anonymous User ID — a random identifier assigned automatically. Not linked to your name or email.
  • Google Display Name — only if you chose to sign in with Google (e.g. "Rohit"). Shown on your Profile screen.
  • Game Progress — your gems balance, best score, highest tile reached, and current board state synced to Firebase Firestore.
✅ We never store: payment details, phone numbers, location, contacts, email addresses, or any sensitive personal information. All purchases are handled entirely by Google Play.

What Gets Deleted vs. Kept

Here is a complete breakdown of what happens to each type of data when you request account deletion:

Data Where Stored On Deletion Timeline
Firebase User ID Firebase Auth Deleted Within 7 days
Game Progress (score, gems, board) Firebase Firestore Deleted Within 7 days
Google Display Name Firebase Firestore Deleted Within 7 days
Local device data (gems, settings) Your Android device Clear manually Instantly (see below)
Purchase history (gem packs, Remove Ads) Google Play & RevenueCat Kept Legal / financial requirement
⚠️ Purchase records cannot be deleted — Google Play and RevenueCat retain transaction records for legal and financial compliance. This is standard across all apps on the Play Store and is outside our control.
📱
Clearing Local Device Data

Your gems, game board, and settings are also stored locally on your device (not just in the cloud). To remove this data:

  • Go to your Android Settings
  • Tap Apps (or Application Manager)
  • Find and tap Money Merge
  • Tap Storage → Clear Data
💡 Tip: Uninstalling the app also removes all locally stored data automatically.
🔧
Deleting Data Without Deleting Your Account

If you'd like to reset your game progress without a full account deletion, you can mention this in your email and we'll clear only your Firestore game data while keeping your account active.

Alternatively, clearing local data via Android Settings (see above) resets your on-device progress immediately — no email required.
